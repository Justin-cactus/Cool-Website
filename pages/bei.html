BEI Precision Systems & Space designs high-reliability encoders for aerospace and defense applications. Embedded within a small engineering team, I contributed across the full FPGA development and validation lifecycle for flight-grade encoder hardware — working mostly in software and firmware while interfacing regularly with lab equipment to verify real hardware behavior.

FPGA Verification & Testbench Development

Designed and built a pre-silicon FPGA verification environment from scratch, establishing the testbench format and structure the team adopted and carried forward. Wrote test cases in Verilog and worked within Libero and Vivado EDA toolchains to simulate and validate design behavior before committing to silicon.

EDAC System Testing & Validation

Tested, calibrated, debugged, and validated the Error Detection and Correction (EDAC) system of the encoder. Work was largely self-directed, using oscilloscopes and logic analyzers to verify signal integrity and system behavior against spec on hardware destined for aerospace environments.

Documentation & Design Validation

Identified and filled significant gaps in existing Design Validation coverage and substantially expanded module-level documentation for the FPGA, improving traceability across the team.

Processor & IP Research

Investigated potential improvements to the current processor architecture and evaluated changes to Actel's SRAM IP using GNU Octave for analysis alongside C/C++ tooling.
