IBM HSIO - Research Page June 2022 - Feb 2025 Austin, Texas

Summary

IBM's I/O Links group develops the high-speed serial interface (Pcie, Ucie, & Several IBM internal Busses) technology powering IBM's Power and Z mainframe product lines. For nearly three years, I worked as a verification engineer embedded within a large org spanning multiple sub-teams, collaborating closely with RTL designers and logic engineers to validate the IoPHY across P-series, Z-series, and Sentient system architectures. My scope and ownership grew steadily throughout — moving from executing and maintaining existing verification work to driving new coverage areas and influencing design decisions directly.

Circuit-Level Functional Verification

Verified behavioral correctness of VHDL logic for a range of analog and mixed-signal circuits within the IoPHY — including CTLE, VGA, Servo Op Interface, PLL, ECC, and FFE blocks. Both maintained existing testbenches and authored new ones from scratch, working in Cadence and Fusion(IBM Internal ~ UVM) simulation environments. Participated in design and verification reviews, contributed to RTL-level debugging, and performed timing analysis in close collaboration with the design team. Findings fed directly into the bug tracking system and in several cases influenced design decisions before tapeout.

Tooling Infrastructure & DevOps

Inherited and maintained the team's internal tooling infrastructure across its full lifecycle before handing it off - keeping automation pipelines healthy and up to date using BASH, TCL, Perl, and CMake. Improvements made to the GUI's and workflows had downstream impact on the broader team's efficiency and reliability.

Documentation

Created comprehensive technical documentation supplemented with custom visuals to improve clarity for both new and existing team members. Presented work and findings regularly to leads and management in a large org environment, adhering to IBM's formal presentation standards and Agile workflow expectations.

Applied AI & NLP Research

Engaged with IBM's AI work across two distinct phases. In late 2022, contributed to an internal outreach initiative aimed at building an accessible NLP education platform for high school students — translating complex machine learning concepts into approachable curriculum material. Later, following OpenAI's commercial breakthrough with ChatGPT, participated in an internal effort to bring IBM's Watson transformer and Granite models back up to competitive standing. Contributed through systematic prompt engineering, experimenting with model weights and parameters, and providing structured evaluation feedback — the kind of hands-on model assessment work that informed how those models were tuned and iterated on during a critical period. Additionally, sat in on early discussions around the Sentient program, which was oriented toward hardware acceleration for AI workloads — bridging the software-side AI work with the hardware verification focus of the core role.
